# NISTIR 6096: historical concrete-anchor vocabulary and evidence gaps

## Evidence identity and strict boundary

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: *Post-Installed Anchors—A Literature Review* is NIST Interagency/Internal Report 6096 by Geraldine S. Cheok and Long T. Phan. The official NIST record and report title page identify the work and its January 1998 publication date. [NIST-PUBREC-6096, identity surface; NISTIR-6096-1998, PDF p. 2]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: NISTIR 6096 is a historical secondary review. It surveys and organizes earlier studies; it is not a new comprehensive NIST experiment, a current standard, or a current field-qualification document. [NIST-PUBREC-6096, abstract surface; NISTIR-6096-1998, abstract / PDF p. 3 and scope / PDF pp. 11–12]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence boundary: Only the NIST employee-authored review synthesis is used. Incorporated standards, third-party studies, quotations, equations, tables, figures, reference bodies, products, numerical results, procedures, and recommendations are excluded. [NISTIR-6096-1998, PDF pp. 1–109; NIST-TECHSERIES-RIGHTS, technical-series rights surface]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence boundary: This digest supplies vocabulary, distinctions among research variables, historical evidence gaps, and internal source leads. It does not approve, select, size, proof-test, certify, install, or evaluate a ceiling attachment, hardpoint, anchor, bondage setup, body-support system, or human-suspension system. [NISTIR-6096-1998, scope / PDF pp. 11–12 and conclusions / PDF p. 81]

## Failure-mode vocabulary in the historical review

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: The review treats failure mode as a classification of how an anchor system or surrounding concrete ceases to carry action. Its summary vocabulary includes steel failure, concrete-cone failure, edge breakout or bursting, splitting, pull-out or slip, bond-interface failure, and combinations of modes. [NISTIR-6096-1998, failure-mode synthesis / printed p. 5, PDF p. 15 and summary / printed p. 67, PDF p. 77]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: Steel failure names failure in the metal anchor component. The label identifies the component where failure occurred; by itself it gives no transferable performance value or acceptance decision. [NISTIR-6096-1998, failure-mode synthesis / printed p. 5, PDF p. 15]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: Concrete-cone failure names a breakout region in the surrounding concrete that forms around an anchorage. The review separates this substrate failure from failure confined to the metal component. [NISTIR-6096-1998, failure-mode synthesis / printed p. 5, PDF p. 15]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: Edge breakout and bursting describe concrete failure associated with proximity to a free edge. They are related edge-sensitive labels, but the review does not collapse every edge-influenced observation into one universal mechanism. [NISTIR-6096-1998, failure-mode synthesis / printed p. 5, PDF p. 15]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: Splitting failure describes cracking that divides or opens the concrete member around the anchorage region. It is distinct from a localized cone or edge-breakout label in the review's taxonomy. [NISTIR-6096-1998, failure-mode synthesis / printed p. 5, PDF p. 15]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: Pull-out or slip names withdrawal or relative movement of the anchor without treating that event as the same mechanism as metal fracture or a full concrete cone. The paired terms are historical vocabulary, not a field diagnostic rule. [NISTIR-6096-1998, failure-mode synthesis / printed p. 5, PDF p. 15]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: For bonded systems, the review distinguishes failure at the anchor-to-bonding-medium interface from failure at the bonding-medium-to-concrete interface. “Bond failure” therefore does not identify one unique interface unless the observation says which interface separated. [NISTIR-6096-1998, failure-mode synthesis / printed p. 5, PDF p. 15 and summary / printed p. 67, PDF p. 77]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: More than one failure mode can appear in combination. The review also states that not every anchor type experiences every listed mode, so the vocabulary is a menu of observed mechanisms rather than a universal sequence. [NISTIR-6096-1998, summary / printed p. 67, PDF p. 77]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: A failure-mode label and a prediction model answer different questions. The label describes the observed mechanism or region; a model attempts to predict response under specified conditions. The review's model comparisons do not make the vocabulary itself predictive. [NISTIR-6096-1998, tension discussion / printed pp. 26–27, PDF pp. 36–37 and prediction summary / printed pp. 69–70, PDF pp. 79–80]

## Distinctions among historical test variables

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: The review organizes research by action regime: tension-only, shear-only, and combined tension-and-shear action. These are experimental classification axes, not operational force specifications or values. [NISTIR-6096-1998, abstract / PDF p. 3 and scope / PDF pp. 11–12]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: It also distinguishes static, cyclic, and reversed-cyclic histories. A result from one history cannot be silently treated as evidence for another history, especially where the review itself calls for further investigation. [NISTIR-6096-1998, summary / printed pp. 67–68, PDF pp. 77–78]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: Cracked and uncracked concrete are separate substrate conditions in the review. The review reports greater variability in the historical cracked-concrete evidence and treats cracked-concrete behavior as an area needing more investigation. [NISTIR-6096-1998, summary / printed pp. 68–70, PDF pp. 78–80]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: Concrete strength, member thickness, embedment, and proximity to an edge are distinct variables. They describe different aspects of the substrate and geometry and should not be substituted for one another in interpreting a study. [NISTIR-6096-1998, general summary / printed p. 67, PDF p. 77]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: Spacing, single-versus-multiple anchorage, and nearby reinforcement describe system context rather than properties of one isolated anchor. The review's inclusion of these variables shows why component evidence and system evidence are not interchangeable. [NISTIR-6096-1998, general summary / printed p. 67, PDF p. 77 and conclusions / printed p. 71, PDF p. 81]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: Direction and eccentricity of action are separate from whether the action is tension, shear, or combined. The historical review tracked these geometry and action distinctions because studies did not all impose the same conditions. [NISTIR-6096-1998, general summary / printed p. 67, PDF p. 77]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: Preload, installation condition, and the presence of other reinforcement appear as additional variables in the review's synthesis. Their presence in the research map establishes that they were studied; it does not provide an installation, adjustment, inspection, or proof-testing procedure. [NISTIR-6096-1998, general summary / printed p. 67, PDF p. 77]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: The report's many study-specific variable sets are not one balanced experiment. They come from different investigators, specimens, methods, and reporting practices, so comparisons across studies retain heterogeneity and missing-data limits. [NISTIR-6096-1998, combined-action discussion / printed pp. 62–63, PDF pp. 72–73]

## Historical evidence gaps and model limits

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: The review was undertaken partly to ask whether the available literature could support a reliable response relationship for post-installed anchors. That framing makes data sufficiency, not merely equation collection, a central question. [NISTIR-6096-1998, introduction / printed p. 1, PDF p. 11]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: In 1998, the review found substantially less evidence for combined tension-and-shear behavior than for tension-only or shear-only behavior, with a particular gap for adhesive anchors. This is a historical finding about the reviewed database, not a statement about the current literature. [NISTIR-6096-1998, abstract / PDF p. 3, combined-action discussion / printed pp. 62–63, PDF pp. 72–73, and summary / printed p. 68, PDF p. 78]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: The authors concluded that the surveyed evidence was insufficient to develop a reliable response relationship for combined action, especially for adhesive anchors. The proposed need for additional experiments documents a gap rather than validating a model. [NISTIR-6096-1998, conclusions / printed p. 71, PDF p. 81]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: The summary also identifies reversed-cyclic behavior and anchors in cracked concrete as areas requiring further investigation. These gaps remain attached to the 1998 review and are not converted here into modern conclusions. [NISTIR-6096-1998, summary / printed p. 68, PDF p. 78]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: In the tension discussion, the review notes that predictive equations were developed for particular condition sets and might not be reliable under different conditions. It also rejects the convenience of one simplified equation when anchor behaviors differ. [NISTIR-6096-1998, tension discussion / printed pp. 26–27, PDF pp. 36–37]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: In the shear discussion, different historical prediction approaches produced inconsistent results across the small comparison set. The review interprets this inconsistency as a need for further model refinement, not as permission to choose the most favorable prediction. [NISTIR-6096-1998, shear discussion / printed pp. 48–49, PDF pp. 58–59]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: The combined-action comparison showed substantial scatter across data assembled from separate studies with varying parameters. The review characterized the fitted interaction as limited and again called for more experimental work. [NISTIR-6096-1998, combined-action discussion / printed pp. 62–63, PDF pp. 72–73]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: Some research known to the authors was ongoing, not yet reported, or omitted because it was proprietary. The report is therefore transparent that its literature coverage was not the complete universe of anchor evidence even at publication. [NISTIR-6096-1998, general summary / printed p. 67, PDF p. 77]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: The report's robustness checks compared predictions with only a small subset of experimental observations. That limited comparison cannot become a current validation claim for any model or anchor system. [NISTIR-6096-1998, prediction summary / printed pp. 69–70, PDF pp. 79–80]

## Internal source-citation leads

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: For terminology, the most direct internal lead is the text-only failure-mode synthesis in the failure-modes section and its restatement in the general summary. The associated figure is excluded and is not required to locate the vocabulary. [NISTIR-6096-1998, printed p. 5 / PDF p. 15 and printed p. 67 / PDF p. 77]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: For historical study discovery, the report separates tension-only, shear-only, and combined-action literature into distinct chapters. Their discussion sections are more useful as evidence-gap leads than the excluded equations and numerical study summaries. [NISTIR-6096-1998, contents / PDF pp. 4–6, discussions / PDF pp. 36–37, 58–59, and 72–73]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: For limitations, the summary and conclusion pages consolidate the review's variable inventory, gaps, model variability, and call for additional evidence. These pages should be read before following any individual citation lead. [NISTIR-6096-1998, summary and conclusions / printed pp. 67–71, PDF pp. 77–81]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence: The bibliography identifies earlier primary and secondary sources across several research traditions. It is retained only as a navigation lead: no cited work's content, authority, rights, or current validity is imported without a separate source audit. [NISTIR-6096-1998, references / printed pp. 73–78, PDF pp. 83–88]

## Current-use boundary

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence boundary: NISTIR 6096 preserves the state and gaps of a literature review published in 1998. It predates current products, standards, assessment methods, and site conditions; nothing in this corpus substitutes for current site-specific qualified engineering verification. [NIST-PUBREC-6096, publication-date and report-identity surfaces; NISTIR-6096-1998, PDF pp. 2–3 and 81]

Non-bondage concrete-anchor evidence boundary: Any real structure requires current governing requirements, site information, material and condition assessment, and decisions by appropriately qualified professionals. This corpus provides no operational selection, sizing, testing, proof-load, or certification claim for a ceiling, hardpoint, anchor, body-support, bondage, or human-suspension application. [NISTIR-6096-1998, historical scope / PDF pp. 11–12 and evidence limits / PDF pp. 77–81]
