# Audit report

## Outcome

The package is accepted as a direct-training Markdown source with `direct_training_ready: true`. The exact official PHM Society PDF was normally accessible and checksumable, and page one gives an article-level Creative Commons Attribution 3.0 United States notice. The retained corpus is a manual, qualitative distillation of article-authored review prose.

The source is a 2017 review in an offshore synthetic-fiber-rope setting. It is not treated as a current survey, a new experiment, or evidence for visual inspection in another rope-use domain.

## Manual review method

The PDF was extracted page by page and rendered page by page. All fourteen pages were read and visually reviewed. Claim-bearing pages were also inspected at readable original resolution. Page dispositions record whether prose was retained in part or the page was excluded.

The visual audit found nineteen numbered figures and no tables. Every figure was attributed to an external source, presented with permission language, or combined external evidence with numerical graphics. All nineteen figures and their captions were excluded. The bibliography-only pages were excluded.

The retained notes were written manually from the article's prose and checked against page images. Citations point to the source pages supporting each claim. The resulting text emphasizes what the review classifies and what it says remains uncertain; it does not upgrade survey coverage into validation.

## Retained information

- Title, authors, year, journal identity, and DOI.
- Offshore synthetic-fiber-rope evidence boundary and 2017 historical framing.
- Filament, yarn, strand, sub-rope, and rope construction hierarchy.
- Need to interpret monitoring in relation to rope construction and structural scale.
- Qualitative interacting damage-mechanism vocabulary.
- Embedded versus nonembedded and continuous versus discrete monitoring axes.
- Technology classes at taxonomy level: detectable markers, conductive sensing, optical sensing, capacitance, vision, geometry, thermography, internal imaging, acoustic emission, vibration, and wave propagation.
- Localization, coverage, integration, environmental, and model-dependence limits.
- Whole-length coverage problem, multisensor rationale, and research gaps as framed in 2017.
- Funding acknowledgement and the absence of reported conflict-of-interest and data-availability statements.

## Excluded information

- Every figure, photograph, plot, diagram, composite, caption, and image-derived claim.
- Third-party or permission-reproduced components regardless of apparent relevance.
- Bibliography entries and reference-only pages.
- Patents, standards, vendors, product identities, trademarks, and proprietary implementation claims.
- Numerical values, equations, thresholds, material rankings, performance rankings, and correlations.
- Discard, remaining-life, repair, retirement, or other operational decision rules.
- Material-transfer examples outside the synthetic offshore scope.
- Any transfer to body loading, human suspension, anchors, uplines, or visual inspection in bondage practice.
