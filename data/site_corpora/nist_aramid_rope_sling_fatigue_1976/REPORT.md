# Curation and rights report

## Outcome

Accepted as a narrow historical-evidence corpus. The retained artifact is a manually written, claim-cited digest of NBSIR 76-1159. No source HTML, PDF, scan, photograph, figure, table, or extracted full text is stored in this directory.

The source is useful because the failed evaluation is unusually instructive: end-fitting inadequacies repeatedly censored the intended rope-body measurements. The digest front-loads that limitation and the 1976 helicopter external-cargo prototype context. It excludes numerical load and rating recipes and blocks transfer to modern rope systems, bondage rope, or human suspension.

## Manual source review

The official NIST landing page and official NIST-hosted PDF were fetched directly on July 16, 2026. The PDF contains 44 scanned PDF pages. Every page was reviewed, with the following manual inventory:

- PDF pages 1–4: cover/title leaves and blank scan pages.
- PDF pages 5–8: contents, table list, figure list, and scan artifacts.
- PDF page 9: abstract and introduction.
- PDF pages 10–16: specimens, rope configurations, jackets, end fittings, preliminary tensile method, source tables, and start of fatigue methods.
- PDF pages 17–23: both fatigue-spectrum paths, source result tables, residual-strength follow-up, and start of simulated weathering.
- PDF pages 24–31: weathering apparatus/results, source tables, permanent elongation, discussion, end-fitting limitations, untested combined effects, and concluding remarks.
- PDF page 32: references.
- PDF pages 33–42: scanned photographs/figures and blank or artifact pages.
- PDF page 43: government bibliographic data sheet.
- PDF page 44: trailing blank/artifact scan.

Text extraction was used only as a finding aid and was manually checked against section order and page content. The scan's extracted text is unreliable for tables and often lacks spaces. It was not promoted into corpus prose. The evidence digest was written manually from the reviewed report sections.

## Bibliographic reconciliation

The current NIST landing page identifies Nixon Halsey and Leonard Mordfin, NBS IR 76-1159, and DOI `10.6028/NBS.IR.76-1159`. It lists January 1, 1976 as the publication date. The report title page and its government bibliographic sheet both state September 1976, so the digest uses September 1976 as the imprinted report date and records the landing-page discrepancy explicitly.

The report describes a final test period in 1975, but that administrative date is not necessary to understand the scientific evidence and is not used as a protocol cue. Obsolete ordering, price, and distribution fields on the bibliographic sheet were excluded.

## Scientific disposition

Included as paraphrased evidence:

- 26 prototype aramid-fiber sling-leg specimens in a 1976 helicopter external-cargo development context.
- Parallel-strand and cabled-strand construction families across low, medium, and high nominal capacity categories, without numerical ratings.
- The existence and qualitative structure of increasing-load and alternating-block fatigue spectra.
- Delivered-spectrum deviations caused by programming malfunction.
- Failure-mode distinctions among free-length damage, partial strand damage, pullout, fitting-adjacent failure, and survival to follow-up.
- Simulated moisture, heat, and ultraviolet exposure, including apparatus/control anomalies.
- Residual-strength follow-up and why most endpoints remained termination-limited.
- Qualitative permanent or quasi-permanent elongation observations and the source's explicit sparse-data warning.
- The decisive limitations: termination inadequacy, sparse specimens, missing comparison cells, mixed jackets/fittings, survivor selection, and separate rather than combined fatigue/weathering exposure.

Excluded:

- All numerical loads, working or breaking ratings, spectrum intensities, and rating-derived recipes.
- Helicopter lifting or external-cargo operating instructions.
- End-fitting dimensions, fabrication, potting recipes, or design recommendations.
- Manufacturer property claims, vendor identity, and proprietary components.
- Source tables, photographs, figures, captions as image substitutes, and apparatus construction details.
- Obsolete procurement, availability, and price information.
- References as a route to unreviewed third-party works.
- Any transfer to bondage rope, natural fibers, knots, uplines, care/retirement rules, body contact, load ratings, or human suspension.

The row-level disposition file records these decisions in deterministic form.

## Rights and component-provenance audit

NIST's current general copyright page says that, except for material marked copyrighted, information on NIST sites is considered public information that may be copied or distributed, and it requests appropriate byline, photo, and image credits. The current NIST Technical Series policy says employee-authored works are not protected by U.S. copyright, reserves foreign rights while granting broad reuse rights, requests the recommended citation plus a NIST courtesy statement for republication, and warns that some NIST-published works may have third-party authors or protected components.

The report title page identifies its authors with the National Bureau of Standards, and NIST currently catalogs the report as a NIST publication. That supports reuse of employee-authored report prose in the United States, but this corpus does not rely on a blanket assumption about every component. This is a provenance screen, not legal advice.

Component cautions found during review:

- The PDF metadata identifies the Internet Archive as digitizer/creator and LuraDocument as PDF producer. The scanned-page and encoding layers therefore have provenance beyond the 1976 report authors.
- The report contains photographs of specimens and an explicitly proprietary experimental fitting. No images or close caption substitutes were retained.
- Some source tables reproduce manufacturer-supplied typical properties or ratings. No table content or vendor claim was retained.
- The specimens and terminations were supplied or installed by manufacturers, but the retained digest reports only the NBS authors' experimental observations and limitations.
- References point to standards and other publications with separate provenance. They were not followed or incorporated.

The corpus uses fresh manual paraphrase, complete attribution, a change notice, and no endorsement claim. It stores URLs, retrieval metadata, hashes, and dispositions rather than protected source artifacts.

## Automated extraction audit

The local review copy was a 44-page PDF. A deterministic extraction pass yielded 45,125 characters and 3,365 alphanumeric tokens, with extraction-stream SHA-256 `ae11afd889783a8f919a4afefe772b4155ba1f8d4913671a000b71f0a4c42219`. This is an audit fingerprint only; the extracted stream is not stored.

The PDF exposes two raster image XObjects on every PDF page, for 88 total. Because the report is a scan, this count describes scan-layer structure and must not be interpreted as 88 authored figures. The source itself lists eight figures and eleven tables. All raster page content, authored figures, and source tables were excluded.

## Quality controls

Deterministic tests verify source identifiers and hashes, metadata reconciliation, source-ledger shape, manual page inventory, citation coverage, prominent limitations, the absence of numerical load units and source artifacts, split hygiene, domain-transfer exclusions, manifest hashes, and row-level dispositions.

This corpus must remain source-only. Do not derive validation, holdout, OOD, probe, or evaluation material from it. Any future Q&A must be generated into a separately reviewed training-only artifact and must preserve the limitation that the rope bodies were not thoroughly evaluated.
