# Manual audit report

## Outcome

The package is accepted as a text-only direct-training source with `direct_training_ready: true`. The exact official NPS PDF was normally accessible and checksumable. The retained prose is National Park Service-authored federal material treated as public domain in the United States. Every figure and every credited, permission-controlled, commercial, or uncertain component is excluded.

## Manual review method

The PDF was extracted and rendered page by page. All fifty physical page images were manually reviewed in ordered contact sheets. All fifty page texts were screened, and physical pages six through seventeen, corresponding to K:2 through K:13, were read at claim level. `dispositions.jsonl` records one decision for every physical page.

The source lists sixteen numbered figures. All sixteen were reconciled to their body pages and excluded, including NPS photographs, permission-reproduced material, credited drawings, diagrams, and courtesy photographs. Three additional controlled blocks cover an in-scope commercial-product claim, external care-resource directions, and the bibliography/resource pages. `components.jsonl` records nineteen categorical exclusions.

## Retained museum-textile mechanisms

- Animal, plant, and synthetic fiber distinctions.
- Protein-chain framing for animal fibers.
- Cellulose chemistry and stem/bast, leaf, and seed groupings for plant fibers.
- Regenerated and laboratory-created synthetic-fiber distinction and 2002 uncertainty about long-term aging.
- Influence of spinning, textile structure, early fiber preparation, dyes, mordants, lubricants, finishes, and embellishments.
- Molecular-chain shortening and brittleness.
- Loss of inherent moisture and declining elasticity in aging natural fibers.
- Hygroscopic moisture exchange, dimensional change, and mechanical stress.
- Qualitative heat, light, ultraviolet, moisture, oxidation, pollution, pest, and abrasion mechanisms.
- Interactions among fibers, finishes, metals, particulate matter, biological activity, and environmental cycling.

Every retained claim is labeled `Museum-textile evidence` and carries a printed-page and physical-PDF citation.

## Exclusions

- Every figure, photograph, drawing, diagram, caption, and image-derived claim.
- Vendor, trademark, and commercial-product matter.
- Bibliography, external resource directions, and externally attributed material.
- Numerical temperature, relative-humidity, and illumination prescriptions.
- Storage, display, pollution-control, pest-control, handling, cleaning, treatment, chemical, transport, emergency, and conservation recipes.
- Direct material-performance transfer to functional rope.
- Any strength, hygiene, conditioning, drying, inspection, service-decision, or retirement inference for rope.
- All references or transfer to bondage, body loading, uplines, anchors, hardpoints, or human suspension.

## Transparency

The appendix identifies no individual author, conflict-of-interest statement, funding statement, or data-availability statement. The corporate author is the National Park Service.

The original PDF is not redistributed. Exact first-party retrieval metadata, checksum, public-domain basis, bibliographic-date resolution, and component policy are preserved in `sources.jsonl` and `source_snapshot/provenance.json`.
