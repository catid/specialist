## Boundary

This pass searched for candidates only. It did not crawl a site, copy corpus text, alter the authoritative site-corpus registry, open protected evaluation data, authorize training, or extend the source-disjoint contract. The candidate registry deliberately includes rejects so weak sources do not re-enter through a later search.

The 32 candidates resolve to:

- 11 `collect` recommendations, all conditional on their recorded reviews.
- 15 `manual-review` recommendations.
- 6 explicit `reject` recommendations.

`collect` means “worth a bounded collection task after prerequisites,” not “currently eligible for the dataset.”

Every candidate row explicitly records its exact search-discovered URL and domain, canonical-resolution status, title and publisher/owner, topics and gap/overlap, authority evidence, estimated/page scope, duplication against the 33-resource registry, rights/license evidence, TOS/robots/AI-access note, safety-transfer flags, recommended collection depth, disposition, and `candidate_only_not_authorized_not_training_eligible` promotion state. An unresolved canonical publisher asset is represented as an unresolved review requirement rather than guessed.

## Best additions

1. **USFS National Tree Climbing Guide (2015)** — the strongest new hardpoint/rigging candidate. It combines tree assessment, anchor selection, friction systems, rope inspection, retirement, and equipment care in one federal guide. It must remain occupational tree-climbing evidence, not a claim that arbitrary trees are safe for bondage suspension.

2. **Army TM 3-34.86, TC 21-24, and the Army Mountain Warfare School Knot Guide** — a useful three-source technical cluster for rigging vocabulary, knots/hitches, splicing, redundant/equalized anchors, inspections, and tying checkpoints. All need edition/currentness checks, and old military load practices must not be presented as modern human-suspension guidance.

3. **Frictional Mechanics of Knots (Research Square, CC BY 4.0)** — the best openly licensed mechanics candidate. It adds analytical treatment of contact, self-locking, and generalized capstan behavior. Its idealized assumptions must travel with every derived fact.

4. **OpenStax Anatomy and Physiology first edition, selected PNS/circulation sections (CC BY 4.0)** — a strong foundation for accurate nerve and circulation terminology. Pin the first edition: later OpenStax edition licensing is different. Anatomy does not create safe rope placements or diagnostic rules.

5. **Effects of Safety Harnesses on the User's Body in Suspension (MDPI, CC BY 4.0)** — useful controlled evidence about strap pressure, posture, and discomfort. It is occupational-harness research with limited participants and short exposure, not a surrogate shibari trial.

6. **Two National Diet Library protection-expired Japanese manuals** — unusually valuable primary material for taihojutsu, jujutsu, and hayanawa history. They can reduce dependence on circular modern lineage summaries. Collection requires NDL policy review, exact PID/page manifests, expert Japanese transcription/translation, and explicit separation of coercive historical restraint from modern consensual practice.

7. **NCSF educational and negotiation resources** — fill an important consent/communication gap. The landing page visibly assigns Creative Commons licenses to particular presentations, but not to the whole NCSF site. Exact asset URLs and per-file licenses must be resolved before any collection.

8. **TheDuchy adaptation page and the BDSM/disability communication study** — the strongest accessibility leads. TheDuchy is practically dense but copyrighted and medically non-authoritative; the peer-reviewed study is credible but not presently open-licensed. Both favor reviewed, citation-backed original synthesis over bulk copying.

9. **BSEE rope-access incident, NPS Dewey Peak fatality report, and Interagency Aviation Bulletin 18-01** — add real failure analysis: device threading, function/buddy checks, rescue planning, changing direction of pull, lost redundancy, slippage, component provenance, and hydrogen embrittlement. Use only minimized, anonymized, fact-focused digests. Do not train on unnecessary names, death narrative, photographs, or organization-specific blame.

10. **NPS Technical Rescue Handbook, 12th edition** — potentially one of the densest sources, but the found copy is a third-party mirror. Locate an official NPS copy or establish bit-identical provenance and component rights before collection.

## Rights and quality surprises

- “Hosted by a government site” is not enough. Student research, contributed diagrams, manufacturer inserts, photographs, and FOIA attachments can retain separate rights.
- ArXiv availability is not itself an open-content license. The 2026 clove-hitch preprint needs its exact submission license checked.
- The peer-reviewed 2022 clove-hitch author PDF is CC BY-NC-ND 4.0. Its no-derivatives condition makes transformed training-corpus use a manual rights decision, despite its technical value.
- The PMC disability/communication article was made reusable under a temporary COVID-era permission that has ended. It currently carries an exclusive Springer license.
- OpenStax edition identity matters: the first edition is visibly CC BY 4.0; do not silently substitute a later edition.
- NCSF licenses are asset-specific. Nearby CC-licensed presentations do not license its negotiation, policy, or “Got Consent” PDFs as a bundle.
