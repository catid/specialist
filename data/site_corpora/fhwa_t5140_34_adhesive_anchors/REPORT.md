## Manual review method

All four physical pages were extracted and read manually. All four rendered pages were visually inspected in one ordered contact sheet. `dispositions.jsonl` records one decision for each physical page: pages one through three are partial and page four is excluded.

The component audit identifies eight categorical exclusions. These include the page branding; incorporated standards; third-party certification-program language; product and installation detail; exact project prescriptions; one structural schematic; exact load, material, mechanics and timeline examples; and one third-party table spanning the last two pages. No visual or table-derived claim is retained.

## Retained civil-infrastructure lessons

- Investigated suspended-ceiling and ceiling-panel collapse context, with an explicit note that the PDF provides no casualty detail.
- Inadequate adhesive-system creep resistance as primary cause and irregular overhead installation as contributing cause in FHWA's failure account.
- Persistent tension and creep as time-dependent system concerns.
- Orientation alone does not determine whether an anchor experiences sustained tension.
- Qualification of the complete anchor system for the intended condition.
- Trained installation as a distinct control for installation-sensitive performance.
- Continuous inspection as a governance requirement, without an inspection recipe.
- Owner responsibility for defining inspector qualifications and sustained-condition guidance.
- Qualified owner processes for inspection, engineering evaluation or testing, and remediation of unresolved existing installations, without procedures or thresholds.

Every retained claim paragraph is labeled `Non-bondage civil-infrastructure evidence` or `Non-bondage civil-infrastructure evidence boundary` and carries a physical-PDF citation.

## Exclusions

- All incorporated standards text, quotations, titles, identifiers, load tables, and classifications.
- Third-party organization and certification-program names, marks, and requirements.
- Every figure, diagram, table, label, calculation, and image-derived claim.
- Every product class, exact material, precise force or load, duration, timeline, and scenario list.
- Installation, inspection, testing, design, retrofit, replacement, or maintenance recipes and thresholds.
- Any certification, sizing, selection, rating, or capacity statement.
- Any claim that the advisory qualifies a DIY ceiling attachment, hardpoint, rope anchor, or human-suspension system.

## Transparency and use boundary

The advisory does not identify an individual author, funding statement, conflict-of-interest statement, or data-availability statement. FHWA is the corporate author.

The four-page advisory identifies an investigated ceiling-panel collapse and provides no casualty detail. The corpus therefore does not characterize consequences beyond the documented collapse.

The original PDF is not redistributed. Exact first-party retrieval metadata, checksums, rights basis, page decisions, and categorical component exclusions are preserved in package metadata.
