# Curation, OCR, evidence, relationship, and rights report

## Outcome

Accepted as a bounded historical-methods corpus. The retained artifact is a manually written, claim-cited digest of a 1989 tensile structural model for eight-strand plaited synthetic marine rope. It preserves the rope/strand/plied-yarn hierarchy, unloaded and deformed geometry, two limiting inter-yarn friction assumptions, constituent-force aggregation, selected inter-strand pressure and relative-motion mechanisms, lateral-contraction sensitivity, and the authors’ qualitative-only conclusion.

The corpus excludes every equation, numerical parameter, plot, photograph, contact-area value, calculation algorithm, rating, strength or fatigue-life prediction, blending suggestion, and design prescription. It makes no transfer to natural fiber, other synthetic constructions, knots, splices, rope care or retirement, field procedures, bondage, body contact, uplines, anchors, hardpoints, or human suspension.

## Scientific boundary audit

The digest distinguishes three scales: eight strands in the rope, a curved representative strand, and plied yarns arranged in core, sublayer, and surface layers. It preserves the model’s nested geometry without turning any dimensions, twist counts, packing counts, or path equations into a manufacturing recipe.

The two friction endpoints are carefully located between plied yarns inside a strand. They are not conflated with relative movement between whole strands at rope contacts. The no-friction endpoint permits along-yarn redistribution; the no-relative-motion endpoint ties local yarn segments to changing geometry. Neither is represented as a measured state of a real rope.

Pressure is retained only as a force-balance mechanism. The report estimates selected contacts using assumed areas and idealized compressive geometry, so all numerical pressures are excluded. Relative motion is retained only as the reported combination of strand sliding and pivoting rotation, with rotation emphasized at selected high-pressure zones. Their overlap supports a qualitative internal-abrasion hypothesis, not a wear-rate or lifetime calculation.

The model ceases to be structurally credible once damage changes load sharing or geometry. The source itself calls its break treatment unrealistic and limits the analysis to damage initiation. This corpus therefore omits breaking-load, breaking-strain, strength, residual-strength, fatigue, and life outputs.

Lateral contraction is the controlling evidence limitation. The source assumes rope and strand contraction are the same while admitting they may differ, does not establish the correct contraction behavior, and concludes that its results can only be qualitative until a satisfactory determination method exists.

## Relationship audit: 1989 versus 1990

The 1989 report says its geometry, pressure, and movement analysis is a first step toward studying deterioration of eight-strand plaited rope. A separately reviewed 1990 MIT Sea Grant report by Seo, Backer, and Mandell develops an internal-abrasion and tensile creep-fatigue deterioration model.

The reports share Stanley Backer, grant `NA86AA-D-SG089`, and project `RT-11`, but they do not model the same rope construction. The 1989 population is eight-strand plaited synthetic rope. The 1990 population is synthetic double-braided rope. The 1990 report’s reference list attributes its structural model to Seo’s 1988 dissertation and does not list the Wang–Backer 1989 report.

The retained relationship is therefore programmatic and conceptual, not a claim that the 1990 model directly extends this exact eight-strand calculation. No 1990 damage equation, parameter, prediction, or service-life content is imported here. The relationship-only source appears in `sources.jsonl` to make this distinction auditable.

## Rights and component screen

The current NOAA record explicitly labels the checksum-bound main document “Public Domain.” The digest is a manual adaptation with different wording, organization, and emphasis. It retains no source page, OCR transcription, equation set, plot, photograph, logo, or table.

Report-level public-domain status does not override the corpus boundary. Source visuals, formula sets, parameter values, plotted results, and design or blending suggestions remain excluded. The relationship-only 1990 report is separately public-domain and is used only for a narrow construction/source-lineage comparison. This is a provenance screen, not legal advice.
