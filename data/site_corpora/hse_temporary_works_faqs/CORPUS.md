## Temporary duration does not reduce criticality

Non-bondage UK construction-governance evidence: The page rejects the idea that work deserves less care merely because it will exist for a short period. Short duration does not make temporary works less important; duration changes how long a construction arrangement remains, not whether failure or loss of control matters. [HSE-TW-FAQ-2026, faq-08-duration-criticality]

Non-bondage UK construction-governance evidence: The governance lesson is about criticality, not permanence. A short service interval is not evidence that design, construction, or coordination risk has become negligible; duration and consequence are different attributes. [HSE-TW-FAQ-2026, faq-08-duration-criticality]

Non-bondage UK construction-governance evidence: “Temporary” therefore should not be used as a reason to weaken attention, omit responsibility, or bypass competent review. This is a paraphrase of the page's construction-management principle, not an acceptance criterion for any particular structure. [HSE-TW-FAQ-2026, faq-08-duration-criticality]

## End-to-end responsibility

Non-bondage UK construction-governance evidence: The person organizing temporary works needs awareness of problems that can arise throughout the process. The retained principle is that responsibility spans the lifecycle and its interfaces rather than ending once one isolated task is assigned. [HSE-TW-FAQ-2026, faq-09-self-organisation]

Non-bondage UK construction-governance evidence: Taking the organizing role oneself does not narrow the role. The page frames the work as something to be coordinated thoroughly and systematically, so unassigned stages or assumptions remain governance gaps rather than somebody else's implicit responsibility. [HSE-TW-FAQ-2026, faq-09-self-organisation]

Non-bondage UK construction-governance evidence: End-to-end responsibility does not mean that one person performs every specialist task. It means the overall process has explicit ownership, appropriate task allocation, and no silent gap between design, site conditions, coordination, use context, and later change. [HSE-TW-FAQ-2026, faq-09-self-organisation and faq-14-design-versus-coordination]

Non-bondage UK construction-governance evidence: A systematic process also depends on handoffs. Information about assumptions, conditions, scope, and role boundaries must reach the people whose decisions depend on it; a task can be individually competent yet still fail at an unmanaged interface. [HSE-TW-FAQ-2026, faq-09-self-organisation, faq-11-site-assumptions, and faq-14-design-versus-coordination]

## Validate assumptions against the site

Non-bondage UK construction-governance evidence: A generic or standard solution remains conditional on its underlying assumptions. Before relying on it in the construction context described by HSE, the organizer needs to establish that the actual site and surrounding conditions fit those assumptions. [HSE-TW-FAQ-2026, faq-11-site-assumptions]

Non-bondage UK construction-governance evidence: A familiar label does not validate a condition. The important distinction is between information supplied for a defined situation and evidence that the present situation actually matches that definition. [HSE-TW-FAQ-2026, faq-11-site-assumptions]

Non-bondage UK construction-governance evidence: Site validation is not a one-time transfer of a generic answer. If the physical context, nearby activity, material condition, or imposed action differs from an assumed case, the basis for using that case has changed. [HSE-TW-FAQ-2026, faq-11-site-assumptions, faq-12-proprietary-load-context, and faq-13-designer-competence]

## Proprietary systems and missed actions

Non-bondage UK construction-governance evidence: Proprietary or supplier-provided systems do not remove the need for competent assessment of relevant loads and conditions. Product information can describe a system, but it does not itself establish that the system fits a particular site or use context. [HSE-TW-FAQ-2026, faq-11-site-assumptions and faq-12-proprietary-load-context]

Non-bondage UK construction-governance evidence: The page warns that relevant actions can be underestimated, especially when actions from the side are not considered. The retained lesson is directional completeness: an assessment limited to the most obvious action can miss a materially different action path. [HSE-TW-FAQ-2026, faq-12-proprietary-load-context]

Non-bondage UK construction-governance evidence: Atypical actions and nearby work are context questions, not afterthoughts. The page's broader discussion connects temporary-works behavior to how a system is used and to other activity nearby, which makes the actual environment part of the assessment boundary. [HSE-TW-FAQ-2026, faq-12-proprietary-load-context and faq-13-designer-competence]

Non-bondage UK construction-governance evidence: The mention of missed side action is not a load value, formula, or sizing rule. It is evidence that an incomplete action inventory can defeat an otherwise familiar system, and that proprietary status is not a substitute for site-specific reasoning. [HSE-TW-FAQ-2026, faq-12-proprietary-load-context]

## Competence and separate roles

Non-bondage UK construction-governance evidence: The FAQ ties temporary-works design competence to relevant training and experience. It does not treat a broad professional label by itself as proof that a person has the specific background needed for this class of construction work. [HSE-TW-FAQ-2026, faq-13-designer-competence]

Non-bondage UK construction-governance evidence: Competence is context-sensitive because temporary works can be affected by their use and by nearby work. The retained principle is to match expertise to the task and its interactions, not to infer competence solely from an unrelated title. [HSE-TW-FAQ-2026, faq-13-designer-competence]

Non-bondage UK construction-governance evidence: Design and coordination are distinct roles. Appointing a designer does not automatically assign the wider coordination function, and responsibility for that function should not be inferred from the design appointment alone. [HSE-TW-FAQ-2026, faq-14-design-versus-coordination]

Non-bondage UK construction-governance evidence: Coordination is broader than producing a design. It addresses when and where temporary works are needed and whether the surrounding process keeps the design, site conditions, work sequence, status, and responsible people aligned. This is a governance distinction, not a setup checklist. [HSE-TW-FAQ-2026, faq-14-design-versus-coordination]

Non-bondage UK construction-governance evidence: Role separation makes interfaces visible. If one party designs and another coordinates, their scopes, information exchange, and decision boundaries need to be explicit; otherwise each can reasonably assume that the other owns a missing task. [HSE-TW-FAQ-2026, faq-09-self-organisation and faq-14-design-versus-coordination]
