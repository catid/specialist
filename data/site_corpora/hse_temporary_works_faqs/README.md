# HSE temporary-works governance corpus

This package is a manually audited, quotation-light digest of the Health and Safety Executive page *Temporary works (TW) FAQs*, whose displayed update date is 11 March 2026.

The package is marked `direct_training_ready: true`. It adapts only eligible Crown textual material from the exact official HSE FAQ under the Open Government Licence. Contains public sector information published by the Health and Safety Executive and licensed under the Open Government Licence.

The HSE copyright page, the official Open Government Licence page, and robots files for both hosts are retained solely as rights and access provenance. The National Archives robots surface records `ai-train=no`; consequently no National Archives licence-page body text enters `CORPUS.md`. HSE logos, all images and illustrations, multimedia, linked-document bodies, standards, third-party components, and uncertain material are excluded.

The corpus is limited to high-level UK construction governance: temporary duration does not reduce criticality; responsibility spans the process; site assumptions require validation; proprietary systems still require competent load-and-condition assessment; side or atypical actions can be missed; competence requires relevant training and experience; and design is distinct from coordination.

It contains no standard text, numerical example, calculation, proprietary product detail, equipment selection, installation or setup recipe, linked-document content, legal advice, design rule, approval, sizing, testing, or certification claim. It is explicitly not evidence for ceiling tension, hardpoints, anchors, uplines, body support, bondage, or human suspension.

Files:

- `CORPUS.md`: claim-cited, explicitly labeled non-bondage UK construction-governance digest.
- `sources.jsonl`: exact identities, retrieval metadata, rights, robots status, and checksums for the five allowed bodies.
- `surfaces.jsonl`: full manual disposition of every material HTML and robots surface.
- `components.jsonl`: controlled component exclusions and partial-retention boundaries.
- `source_snapshot/provenance.json`: exact retrieval, displayed-date resolution, rights basis, robots signals, and audit record.
- `REPORT.md`: method, retained principles, exclusions, rights analysis, and current-use limit.
- `manifest.json`: schemas, readiness flag, statistics, and artifact hashes.
- `tests/test_corpus.py`: deterministic integrity, scope, rights, and leakage checks.

The five allowed source bodies are not redistributed. Their official URLs and exact checksums are recorded for independent verification.
