# Manual audit report

## Outcome

This package is accepted as a quotation-light, bounded HSE governance corpus with `direct_training_ready: true`. It uses one official HSE construction FAQ as technical source and four narrowly scoped rights/access bodies: the HSE copyright page, the official Open Government Licence page, and robots files for both hosts. No site crawl, mirror, archive, search snippet, or linked technical document was used.

The FAQ's displayed update date is 11 March 2026. Its later HTTP `Last-Modified` value is recorded as hosting provenance only. The package preserves the displayed content date rather than silently substituting a server timestamp.

## Rights and robots determination

The HSE copyright page says website information is Crown copyright unless otherwise indicated and makes Crown material available under the Open Government Licence. It also provides a preferred HSE acknowledgement and warns that some images, illustrations, multimedia, and logos are outside the general reuse permission.

The official Open Government Licence page confirms version 3.0 rights and conditions, but its body is used only to verify licence provenance. The National Archives robots file states `Content-Signal: ai-train=no, search=yes, ai-input=no`. No wording or substantive content from that licence page is included in `CORPUS.md`. Its robots signal and its absence from the training text are deterministic test conditions.

The HSE robots file does not disallow the FAQ or copyright route and contains no AI content signal. The package therefore adapts only eligible HSE Crown text, excludes every image, logo, third-party or uncertain component, and supplies the preferred acknowledgement: Contains public sector information published by the Health and Safety Executive and licensed under the Open Government Licence.

## Manual review method

The exact FAQ HTML was manually reviewed from document head through template end. The audit covered metadata, scripts, cookies and analytics, SVG branding, header and global navigation, breadcrumb, heading, all eight accordion questions and their paragraphs, related links, sidebar and back-to-top controls, feedback, footer navigation, licence notice, displayed update date, scripts, and template comments.

The HSE copyright HTML and official Open Government Licence HTML were likewise reviewed from head through footer for rights provenance only. Both robots bodies were reviewed line by line. `surfaces.jsonl` records one decision for every material surface; `components.jsonl` reconciles visual assets, scripts, standards and legal wording, numerical and calculation matter, products and equipment, operational process detail, linked bodies, contact/feedback controls, and other controlled components.

## Retained high-level governance

- Temporary duration does not make work less critical.
- Organizing responsibility spans the process and its handoffs.
- Generic or standard-solution assumptions must be validated against actual site conditions.
- Proprietary or supplier-provided systems still require competent assessment of relevant actions and conditions.
- Side actions, atypical actions, nearby activity, and other context can be omitted if the assessment boundary is too narrow.
- Competence depends on relevant training, experience, and task context.
- Design and coordination are different roles; a design appointment does not silently assign the broader coordination function.

These are paraphrased as UK construction governance, not as formulas, acceptance criteria, or universal legal statements.

## Exclusions

- All standards text, standard identifiers, legal wording, regulatory interpretation, and universal-law claims.
- Every numerical example, value, dimension, calculation, table, formula, coefficient, load value, capacity, threshold, and sizing rule.
- Every proprietary name, product example, equipment type, supplier direction, and equipment-selection claim.
- Every installation, assembly, setup, checking, loading, maintenance, removal, testing, proof-load, design, and certification recipe.
- Every linked publication and external-site body; links are dispositioned but not followed except the explicitly allowed rights licence.
- All logos, SVG paths, icons, images, illustrations, multimedia, visual styling, and image-derived claims.
- All navigation, analytics, scripts, cookies, search, feedback, contact, and template infrastructure.
- All third-party or uncertain material.

## Current-use and safety boundary

This package is non-bondage UK construction-governance evidence. It is not a design, approval, selection, sizing, installation, setup, testing, proof-load, or certification source for ceiling tension, hardpoints, anchors, uplines, body-support systems, bondage systems, or human-suspension systems.

Nothing here replaces current project-specific requirements, verified site information, appropriate role allocation, or appropriately competent professional judgment. The five allowed bodies are not redistributed.
