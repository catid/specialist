# Curation, evidence, and rights report

## Outcome

Accepted as a bounded, training-source-only experimental digest. The retained Markdown is a manual adaptation of a 2020 static loop-knot study. It prioritizes experimental scope, specimen identity and history, loading geometry, replication, ratio uncertainty, failure-mode classification, imaging observations, and stated limitations.

The corpus deliberately does not reproduce the paper’s knot-efficiency table or rankings. Those are especially prone to unsafe transfer because the authors found that efficiency varies with the tested rope’s unknotted static breaking strength. No source body, image, table, formula block, tying diagram, or extracted article text is retained.

## Manual page review

The official publisher PDF contains 17 PDF pages corresponding to printed pages 404–420. Every page was reviewed in sequence:

- PDF pages 1–2 / printed 404–405: title, authors, received/accepted dates, abstract, introduction, prior-method critique, and study controls.
- PDF pages 3–4 / printed 406–407: terminology, rope and cord inventories, histories, tensile apparatus, infrared method, electron microscopy, source Tables I–IV.
- PDF pages 5–6 / printed 408–409: straight-rope variability, ratio-distribution statistics, confidence interval construction, load and geometry definitions, and start of figure-eight evidence.
- PDF pages 7–12 / printed 410–415: results for all eight loop-knot families, replication statements, source figures, ranking Table V, and start of microscopy/thermal results.
- PDF pages 13–14 / printed 416–417: infrared observations, causal uncertainty, electron-microscopy hypothesis, and future-research limits.
- PDF pages 15–17 / printed 418–420: general conclusions, acknowledgments, funding, and references.

Text extraction was used only as a navigation and audit aid. All corpus prose was manually written and checked against section headings, source tables, and explicit limitations. Equations, references, standards language, and operational claims were not adopted.

## Scientific inclusion decisions

Included as manual paraphrase:

- The expert-controlled tying/dressing setting, coupled with the paper’s failure to identify tyers or quantify dressing variability.
- Eight loop-knot families: figure-eight loop, double figure-eight loop, figure-nine loop, overhand loop, bowline, left-hand bowline, bowline on a bight, and alpine butterfly loop.
- Neutral source codes, standard class, diameter, manufacturing year, mission, and recorded wear state for six ropes and one accessory cord.
- The fact that the common all-knot reference specimen was one new type-A rope.
- Slow-static apparatus, friction gripping, polished-ring fixture, stable lab conditions, independent calibration, and instrument-error statement.
- Standard load, cross-load, ring-load, and I/O geometry as experimental labels rather than tying directions.
- The distinction between rupture, partial evidence, survival, and untying.
- Per-claim replication counts and whether one or several ropes were represented.
- Ratio-distribution reasoning, asymmetric uncertainty, and why a ratio of means is not generally the mean ratio.
- The observed dependency of efficiency on unknotted rope strength for two knot families, with no generalization beyond them.
- Infrared observations, measured emissivity, electron-microscopy morphology, and unresolved thermal causality.
- Every limitation stated in the future-research section that constrains transfer.

Excluded:

- Numerical knot efficiencies, retained-strength percentages, rankings, portable comparison deltas, ring-load force thresholds, and regression equations.
- Safe-knot recommendations, load/rating recipes, field procedures, tie-in advice, glacier-travel advice, anchor design, and life-critical prescriptions.
- Storage-life, lifespan, retirement, inspection, and care conclusions.
- Natural-fiber, bondage, body-contact, dynamic, cyclic, upline, or human-suspension transfer.
- Source figures 1–31, source Tables I–V, formulas, and graphical probability distributions.
- Product trademarks, manufacturer identities, declared vendor performance values, equipment brands/models, and commercial support details.
- Standards text, requirements, third-party quotations, literature claims, acknowledgments, funding boilerplate, and references as unreviewed sources.

## Rights and component audit

The journal’s current official about page states that all APPA articles are licensed under Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International. The article PDF itself does not display a Creative Commons notice, so this rights determination is anchored to the current publisher policy and its direct CC BY 4.0 link, with retrieval metadata preserved.

The CC BY 4.0 deed permits sharing and adaptation, provided appropriate credit is given, the license is linked, and changes are indicated; it also bars additional legal or technical restrictions. The corpus includes title, authors, journal/source, DOI, license link, and an explicit adaptation notice. It does not claim endorsement. This is an operational provenance screen, not legal advice.

Potentially distinct or sensitive components were excluded even though the article is licensed:

- Figures include knot depictions, thermal images, microscopy, and apparatus/product context.
- Tables include product trademarks, manufacturers, standards classifications, vendor-declared values, and numerical rankings.
- The article quotes and paraphrases third-party books, standards, and prior studies.
- Equipment models and product marks may carry trademark rights not granted by copyright licensing.
- Acknowledgments identify a rope manufacturer that supplied material and technical support.

Neutral factual specimen codes and histories were manually selected from the tables because they are necessary to interpret the experiment; the tables and vendor performance claims were not reproduced. No statement here implies that CC licensing grants trademark, patent, privacy, publicity, or endorsement rights.

## Split hygiene and quality controls

This package is training-source-only. It must not seed validation, holdout, evaluation, OOD, or probe material. Any future Q&A derived from it must live in a separately reviewed training artifact and must keep specimen identity, load geometry, failure mode, and uncertainty attached to each claim.

Deterministic tests verify bibliography, source hashes, rights attribution, specimen inventory, all eight knot families, replication counts, apparatus boundaries, statistical interpretation, break-versus-untying, imaging uncertainty, limitations, absence of rankings and load recipes, component exclusions, split hygiene, and manifest integrity.
