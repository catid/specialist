## Manual audit method

The PDF was extracted and rendered one physical page at a time. All 116 page images were visually inspected in ordered contact sheets; all 116 page texts were individually screened; and the pages supporting retained claims were read at full readable resolution. `dispositions.jsonl` records exactly one decision per physical PDF page and maps printed report pages where applicable.

The contents and component indexes were reconciled against the body. The report contains 41 numbered figures and 5 numbered tables and lists 16 annexes. All are excluded, regardless of apparent authorship. The separate annex bundle was not opened because the task excludes every annex. A further 27 credited source blocks cover operator records, vendor material, laboratory and test reports, industry guidance, standards, and external casualty reports. Those blocks are also excluded. `components.jsonl` contains 89 auditable exclusion records.

## Retained Crown-authored synthesis

- Exact report identity, corporate author, issue date, and historical scope.
- Industrial LNG-carrier ship-mooring context and HMPE material specificity.
- Long-lay, low-twist load-bearing core enclosed by a tightly fitted braided jacket.
- Jacket protection versus hidden-core observability tradeoff.
- Axial compression within individual components even while the rope is in overall tension.
- Interaction among cyclic loading, bending, twisting, contact, internal friction, heat, abrasion, and localized kinking.
- Coupled load-path framing across line, tail, fittings, routing, and environment.
- Line/fitting incompatibility and failure to evaluate the mooring arrangement as a complete system.
- External visual-inspection and tactile-stiffness blind spots.
- Historical absence of a recognized whole-rope nondestructive condition method in the report's 2017 framing.
- Fragmented operating data and incomplete builder/supplier/operator information exchange.
- Inappropriate direct transfer of offshore test concepts to ship-mooring boundary conditions.
- Case-study methods, uncertainty, and limits on generalization.
- Crown copyright/OGL attribution and absence of reported conflict, funding, or data-availability statements.

## Exclusions

- All incident narrative beyond the minimum report identity and industrial evidence boundary.
- Every person or organization identity not essential to attribution or report identity.
- Every photograph, figure, diagram, plot, table, caption, logo, annex, and image-derived claim.
- Credited third-party, vendor, operator, laboratory, test, and industry material.
- Standards and legal text.
- Equations, measurements, loads, nominal breaking values, ratios, dimensions, time-in-service values, quantitative capacity claims, and thresholds.
- Product names, trademarks, manufacturer advice, and proprietary claims.
- Snap-back-zone procedures and all other operational procedures.
- Rope-selection, care, inspection, service-decision, or retirement recipes.
- All transfer to natural materials, body loading, uplines, anchors, hardpoints, or any human-suspension setting.

## Training boundary

The corpus explicitly states that this single industrial maritime failure does not validate or certify bondage rope or human suspension. It discusses why visual inspection was insufficient in this jacketed HMPE ship-mooring case; it does not turn that finding into advice for another rope material, construction, or use.

No individual author, conflict-of-interest statement, funding statement, or data-availability statement is reported in the source. The corporate author is MAIB. The original PDF and separate annex bundle are not redistributed; exact first-party URLs, response metadata, checksums, and rights evidence are preserved in the package.
